ISB Venture Capital Programme: Choosing the right exit strategy for a VC

In India, the venture capital market has seen a surge in investments in the last decade. With the rise in start-ups, VCs have a greater number of exit opportunities. However, effective exit strategies are critical to realising the returns on investment. Especially since one of the key objectives of venture capitalists (VCs) is to exit their investments with a handsome return on investment.

Programmes like the Venture Capital and Private Equity programme by ISB Executive Education are here to aid VCPE managers with choosing the right exit strategy for their investments.

Here are the top exit strategies for VCs:

1. Initial Public Offering (IPO): Going public is one of the most common exit strategies. With a successful IPO, VCs can sell their shares to the public and make a considerable return on investment. The Indian government has introduced several initiatives, such as the BSE Start-ups Platform, to facilitate the listing of start-ups on the Bombay Stock Exchange. Additionally, the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) has simplified the regulations for the listing of start-ups to make it easier for them to go public. You can learn about more effective exit strategies through ISB Executive Education’s Venture Capital and Private Equity programme.
2. Merger and Acquisition (M&A): M&A is another popular exit strategy. The M&A activity in India has seen a surge in the last decade, with several large companies acquiring start-ups to enhance their product offerings or expand their market share. This has created a favourable environment for VCs looking to exit their investments. By selling their portfolio companies to a larger company, VCs can realise a considerable return on investment. Additionally, the acquisition of start-ups by large corporations can provide the start-up with a platform to scale up and compete globally.
3. Secondary Sale: VCs sell their stake in a portfolio company to another investor. This strategy is typically used when a portfolio company is not yet ready to go public or when there is no imminent M&A opportunity. Secondary sales can provide VCs with an attractive exit option while allowing the portfolio company to continue to grow with new investments. For any potential VCs, it is thus important to understand how to evaluate exit options, growth imperatives and what is in it for you as a VC. The VCPE Programme by ISB Executive Education has key modules such as this and more in its 24-week curriculum.
4. Buyback: A buyback is a strategy used when a portfolio company buys back shares from investors, including VCs. This strategy is commonly used when a portfolio company has significant cash reserves and can afford to buy back shares from investors. A buyback provides VCs with a quick and efficient exit, and the portfolio company can retain its ownership structure.

In conclusion, VCs in India have a range of exit strategies to choose from, depending on the needs of the portfolio company and market conditions. VCs should be aware of the risks associated with each strategy and should work closely with the portfolio company to identify the most effective exit strategy. By using the right exit strategy, VCs can maximise their returns and provide the start-up with a platform to scale up and compete globally.
